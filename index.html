The pre-launch problem

An empty staging app is not a tested staging app.

You can render every screen, write every test, and still discover on launch day that the signup form breaks at scale, the order book skews when 400 users place trades in the same minute, and the recommendations feed has nothing to recommend because nobody's ever bought anything in staging.

  • [ × ]

    Staging is empty

    No users. No history. No one's clicked anything. Your QA flows aren't exercising your app the way real customers will.

  • [ × ]

    Seed data is brittle

    Ten hand-crafted "test users" don't catch race conditions, hot-spot keys, or feed algorithms that fail under skewed populations.

  • [ × ]

    Load tests aren't behavior tests

    Hammering one endpoint with k6 tells you about throughput. It doesn't tell you what happens when 30k people actually use your product.

  • [ × ]

    Demos look dead

    Investor demos, design reviews, partner walkthroughs. A populated staging environment is the difference between "coming soon" and "here's what it looks like."

How it works

Three steps from empty to populated.

Point OracleBot at a staging URL you own. Pick or design personas. We populate, exercise, and report — without ever touching production.

01 / Authorize

Verify the target.

Drop a DNS TXT record or sign in with OAuth so we know it's your staging environment. We refuse to run against domains we can't prove you own.

# oraclebot.net — verify.txt
TXT staging.acme.dev
  "oraclebot-verify=8f3c..."
02 / Choose personas

Pick a population.

Start from a vertical template — trader archetypes, marketplace buyers/sellers, social creators, B2B teams — or compose your own with locale, demographic, and behavior profiles.

personas:
  template: trader-archetypes
  size: 2400
  mix: { scalper: 0.4, swing: 0.4, hodl: 0.2 }
03 / Run & observe

Watch them use your app.

Personas sign up, fund, browse, post, message, and check out at the rates you set. We surface every 4xx, every timeout, every flow that broke — piped into Slack, Linear, or your tool of choice.

$ oraclebot run --rate 200rpm --duration 4h
→ 39 orders · 0.4% 4xx · 1 flow flagged

Commitments

What OracleBot will never do.

Some constraints are how the product works. These are how the product stays the product.

  • ◆

    Never run against production.

    OracleBot refuses targets that look like live production environments. Every run requires a verified staging or pre-launch target — DNS TXT or OAuth proof — and we monitor for production tells (live TLS chain on apex, real user traffic, public DNS) and abort the run if we see them.

  • ◆

    Never run against a domain you don't own.

    Verification is gating, not a checkbox. No verified ownership, no run. We will not "make an exception" because a deal is on the line — the constraint is the product.

  • ◆

    Never inflate metrics for any platform we don't operate.

    OracleBot is a pre-launch readiness tool. It is not a service for boosting signups, followers, transactions, or any other number on a platform someone else runs. Every run is logged with the verified target and operator identity; abuse is investigable and we cooperate with takedown requests against operators who circumvent our verification.

  • ◆

    Never sell or share what we observe.

    What your synthetic users do in your staging environment stays in your account. We retain run logs (request counts, error rates, flagged flows) for the duration of the run and a configurable window after — and that's it. No training corpora, no benchmarks across customers, no leaks.

These commitments are written into our terms and enforced in the runner. If we ever loosen them, you'll see it in a changelog before it ships.
