# Follow-up cadence templates

These cover the gap between "you sent the cold DM" and "they're a
signed design partner." Use exactly the cadence below — more frequent
becomes spam, less frequent loses momentum.

## After cold DM, no reply

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| 0 | Cold DM sent (per [cold-dm.md](./cold-dm.md)) |
| 3 | Follow-up A — "one specific thing" |
| 8 | Follow-up B — "permission to stop" |
| 30 | Move to quarterly nurture (only if they engaged with content) |

### Follow-up A (day 3) — one specific thing

> Quick add to my note from Monday — went back through your scan and
> noticed [one specific finding you didn't mention in the first DM].
>
> [Brief one-line description of what fires it / what the impact is.]
>
> Happy to walk through how the probe works if it's useful — or feel
> free to ignore. Either way, the report's still here:
> [oraclebot.net/score/<id>](…)

**Why this works:** new information, not a re-pitch. You're not asking
for anything; you're giving them more value. Conversion bump is real
because they may have missed your first DM in the noise.

### Follow-up B (day 8) — permission to stop

> Last note from me — totally fine if this isn't a fit, just want to
> make sure I'm not being annoying.
>
> If now isn't the right time but it might be later, would you be open
> to me checking back in 90 days? If not, no problem — I'll stop.

**Why this works:** explicit permission to say no is more likely to
get a yes. Even a "yes, check back in Q3" is information for your
pipeline. Don't write again until day 90.

## After demo call, design partner agreement pending

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| 0 | Demo call done; thank-you note sent within 30 minutes |
| 3 | Follow-up C — "one thing I forgot to show" |
| 7 | Follow-up D — "the one yes I need" |
| 14 | Final note — quarterly nurture if no |

### Thank-you note (day 0, within 30 min)

> [First name],
>
> Thanks for taking the time today. Notes from our call:
>
> – Top finding to fix first: [the highest-severity finding]
> – Suggested next step: [their decision — sign DP agreement / think
> about it / re-scan in 2 weeks]
> – Their best feedback: [the verbatim quote you wrote down]
>
> Two things on my plate from the call:
>
> 1. [Action item you took on, e.g. "send the design-partner agreement"]
> 2. [Action item you took on, e.g. "look into adding a Slack webhook"]
>
> If anything else came up after we hung up, just hit reply.
>
> [Your name]

**Why this works:** zero pitch. You're modeling the recap they wish
their other vendors did. Builds trust without selling.

### Follow-up C (day 3) — one thing I forgot to show

> Forgot to show this on the call — [a specific feature relevant to
> something they asked about]. Two-minute video / GIF here: [link to
> a quick Loom or screen recording].
>
> No need to reply, just thought you'd find it useful. Still have you
> on the [day-7 calendar block / pencilled in for next week] if you
> want to keep the convo going.

**Only send if:** they actually expressed interest in something
specific on the call. Don't send a generic "here's another feature"
note — that screams template.

### Follow-up D (day 7) — the one yes I need

> Quick check-in from last week. Two paths:
>
> **A.** You sign the one-page design-partner agreement → I onboard
> you tomorrow → first re-scan + feedback session in 2 weeks.
>
> **B.** This isn't quite right for you right now → totally fine,
> just want to know so I can re-prioritise.
>
> Either's a yes I can act on. What works for you?

**Why this works:** binary close. Removes the "let me think about
it" purgatory. The ask is for clarity, not a sale.

### Final note (day 14)

> [First name] — pulling back on this one. Totally fine if now isn't
> the time. Two things before I do:
>
> 1. Your readiness badge stays live as long as you want it. If you
> ever want to embed it, the markdown's at [link].
> 2. If you ever ship something new and want a re-scan, just hit
> reply. No agreement needed.
>
> Wishing [project name] all the best.
>
> [Your name]

**Why this works:** generous exit, leaves the relationship open,
costs you nothing. Reactivation rate from "graceful no" emails is
~5%, which is higher than cold outreach.

## After design partner is signed

The first 30 days are the make-or-break period.

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| 0 | Sign agreement, mint org, send onboarding email |
| 1 | Slack/email check-in — "did you get logged in OK?" |
| 7 | First check-in call — review new findings |
| 14 | Async update — "here's what I shipped this week based on what
you said" |
| 21 | Second check-in call |
| 28 | NPS-style ask: "would you recommend us to one specific person?"
If yes, ask for the intro. |

The single most important habit: **ship something every 2 weeks that's
directly traceable to design-partner feedback**. Send them the changelog
with their name attached:

> "[First name] — wired up the Slack webhook you asked about on
> 2026-05-15. Live as of today. Want me to walk through it on next
> week's call?"

Five design partners × 2-week iteration loop = 10 customer-driven
shipments per month. That's faster than any roadmap meeting.
