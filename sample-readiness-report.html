Executive summary

The staging exchange handled steady-state order flow within SLO, but population-scale interaction between high-cancel scalpers and a burst of market orders from panic sellers produced transient order-book divergence and elevated 503s on the cancel path. A single-threaded seed script or one-at-a-time QA journey would not have exercised the contention window.

Finding A1 — Order book divergence under burst (launch risk)

At T+41m, personas scalper_1204–scalper_1209 sustained ~180 cancels/sec while panic_0891 and cohort placed market sells into a thinning bid. For 14 seconds, REST depth at BTC-USD lagged websocket-derived depth by 0.28–0.41 bps notional; one shard returned HTTP 500 on DELETE /api/orders/{id} (12% of cancel attempts during the window).

Why seed scripts miss it: scripts typically cancel one order per loop from a single session; they do not interleave burst cancels with correlated market flow from other personas sharing the same matching partition.

Severity: Launch-blocking for a venue advertising real-time book fidelity.

Headline recommendations

  • Harden cancel path under load: fix 500s on shard ord-svc-3; add backpressure or idempotent cancel retries (detail in full engagement appendix).
  • Add cross-check between websocket book snapshots and REST depth during reconciliation; alert when divergence > 0.25 bps for >2s.
  • Re-run after patch with the same persona mix; OracleBot Standard includes a −30% re-run within 30 days when shipped from the readiness engagement.
